Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about working with us.

What kind of work do you take on?

Custom software development, systems modernization, workflow automation, platform architecture, and data integration. Most of our clients come to us because something in their current setup is not working the way it should, whether that is outdated software, a manual process that has outgrown the team, or a system that has become too fragmented to maintain. If you have a problem like that, we are likely a good fit.

How long does a typical project take?

It depends heavily on scope. A focused automation project might take a few weeks. A full platform build takes longer. We give you a realistic timeline after our first conversation and keep you updated throughout. We do not pad timelines.

Do you work with small businesses or just larger organizations?

Both. We have worked with university research institutes and we are currently building platforms for multi-tenant business networks. Size is not what determines whether we can help. The problem does.

We already have an existing system. Can you work with it?

Yes. Most of the work we do involves existing systems, modernizing them, cleaning them up, connecting them to other tools, or replacing the parts that no longer work. We spend time understanding what you have before we suggest anything.

How do you handle data security?

We take security seriously throughout the build, not just at the end. That means proper access controls, secure data handling, and configurations that follow current best practices. We work within your existing security requirements where they exist.

What happens after the project is done?

You own what we build, and we make sure it is documented well enough that your team can operate it. We are available for support and future work, but we do not build things that require us to maintain them indefinitely just to stay functional.

How is pricing structured?

We scope projects and price them based on what they actually require. After our first conversation, we will give you a clear breakdown before anything starts. No surprises.

Do you use AI in your work?

Sometimes. We are very familiar with current AI tooling and use it when it genuinely fits the problem. We do not use it as a selling point or add it to projects where it does not help. If AI is relevant to what you are trying to build, we will tell you how and why.
